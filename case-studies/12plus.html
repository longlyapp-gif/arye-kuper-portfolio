The Problem

Three masters, one experience.

12+ had to serve Channel 12's live broadcast, a VOD library of hundreds of shows, and short-form clips — while also sustaining itself commercially through advertising. Every design decision sat at the intersection of three competing forces: user delight, editorial goals, and advertising revenue.

The existing experience felt fragmented. Different sections had different visual logic. The content hierarchy wasn't clear. And on smaller screens, the experience broke down completely.

The Approach

Focus on what matters — clear hierarchy on every screen.

Strategy — We created a unified content model that prioritized live, VOD and clips differently based on context and user intent. The advertising was designed to feel like content — not interruption.

Emotion — We identified that 12+ users are in a lean-back state. They want to be guided, not searched. The design creates a sense of discovery and comfort — like channel surfing, elevated.

System — One component library that scales from phone to Smart TV. The same design logic works across 4 different surfaces without breaking.

Product Strategy

Advertising Features.

We developed a dual-screen feature enabling real-time user voting during live broadcasts, a pause-ad that activates when content is paused, and an emotion-based recommendation engine — combining seamless UX with advanced ad monetization.

Product Design

Interface Updates.

A redesigned 404 screen, automatic blocking for overseas users, and an upgraded payment flow — all driven by user feedback and the need to deliver a polished, consistent experience across all platforms.

Design System

A Consistent Design Language.

A comprehensive style guide and design system built to support multiple teams across platforms — standardized templates for each page type, making iterations faster and ensuring a high level of visual consistency.

Impact

A precise, directed viewing experience.

Users felt guided rather than lost. Content was discovered more naturally. The ad experience became less intrusive. And the design system made future product iterations significantly faster.
