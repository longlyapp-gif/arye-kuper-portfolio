Longly

Connecting today to tomorrow.

Role

Lead Product Designer

Scope

End-to-end Product Design

Platform

iOS, Android

The Problem

Task managers track tasks. But humans need meaning.

Most productivity apps suffer from the same fundamental problem: they optimize for task completion, not for human motivation. They show you WHAT to do but not WHY it matters. So users complete tasks and still feel empty.

Longly was born from one question: what if the app could make the invisible connection between small daily actions and the bigger life you're building?

The Approach

Design around the 'why'.

Strategy — Every task in Longly is anchored to a long-term goal. The hierarchy is inverted: you start with the vision, then break it down. Tasks serve goals. Goals serve life.

Emotion — We designed for the feeling of progress, not just the fact of it. Visual systems that show you how far you've come, not just how far you have to go. Celebrating small wins in ways that feel genuine, not gamified.

System — A goal tree structure that connects every micro-task to a macro vision. The architecture is simple enough to use daily, deep enough to reflect a life.

Impact

Users see progress, not just tasks.

Longly users reported a fundamentally different relationship with their goals. Instead of feeling overwhelmed by an endless list, they felt connected to a path. The act of completing a small task became meaningful because its connection to something larger was always visible.

Takeaway

Productivity is not about doing more. It's about understanding why what you're doing matters — and designing that connection into every interaction.
Clarity is a design decision.
