N12

Clarity within information overload.

Role

Product Designer

Scope

Design System, UX, Product Concept

Platform

Web + Mobile

The Problem

News that overwhelms instead of informs.

N12 is one of Israel's leading news platforms. The challenge: an information environment flooded with breaking news, political updates, sports, culture and economy — all competing for the same attention.

The noise was creating cognitive overload. Users couldn't find what mattered. The hierarchy was unclear. The emotional weight of each story was equal — which means nothing felt urgent, and nothing felt calm.

The Approach — Human-Centered Clarity

Three layers of design decision.

Strategy — We defined a clear content hierarchy: breaking news, top stories, and contextual content. What matters most gets maximum visual weight.

Emotion — Every story type has a visual signature. Hard news feels sharp and urgent. Background features feel spacious and calm. The typography and color communicate before you read a word.

System — A scalable design system with defined components for every content state: breaking alerts, editorial packages, live updates, and data visualizations.

Design

Content based on data and events.

We introduced a dual-ball mechanism and delayed ad display to create a smarter, less intrusive advertising experience. Content adapts dynamically to breaking events, keeping the feed relevant without overwhelming the reader.

Product Design

Custom components.

Modernizing the app with a focus on advertising tools — building custom components that support both editorial content and monetization without compromising the reading experience.

Product Concept

Unique website for N12.

Special projects that pushed the platform beyond a standard news site — including a Yom Kippur interface that strips away all noise, and a Flashback game that turned archival content into an engaging user experience.

Design System

Maintaining a Consistent Design Language.

Creating guidelines and standards that allow multiple teams to work simultaneously — ensuring visual and functional consistency across every touchpoint, from breaking news banners to long-form editorial layouts.

Impact

News that leads to understanding, not overload.

Users reported a significantly clearer sense of what was important. The cognitive load reduced. Engagement with contextual content increased as users felt oriented rather than bombarded.

Takeaway

In a world of infinite content, the most powerful design act is deciding what NOT to show — and trusting the user to want less noise, more meaning.
Clarity is a design decision.
